Singaporean Food

History of Singaporean Food

In 1918, Singapore was founded as an international shipping port. Due to this, Singapore's cuisine has been influenced by many different cultures

Singapore is located in between the Pacific and Indian oceans, surrounded by many Asian countries such as Indonesia, China, Malaysia and others. As such, the food and culture in Singapore is also just as diverse

Although Raffles arrived in Singapore in 1819, it is believed that some popular Singaporean food known today orignated before he arrived. It is difficult to know when these dishes actually arrived in Singapore, since historical records on said food are scattered and likely inaccurate.

However, the creation of some Singaporean dishes are likely adaptations from early Singapore immigrants making dishes with their available ingredients, as well as suiting their food palette. These dishes are now well known dishes in Singaporean Cuisine, which includes dishes such as Curry Fish Head, and Kaya Toast.

A large part of Singaporean Cuisine revolves around hawker centers. There is still many of them seen to this day, and they can be traced back all the way to the mid 19th-century. Back then, the vendors was relatively easy to set up, since they could set these up on the side of the roads with either push carts or bicycles. They were also able to serve food that was fast and cheap to many people.

However, due to the lack of infrastructure supporting these vendors, such as waste disposal and a steady supply of fresh water, these stalls were unhygenic in the past. This has changed over the years, the most prominent change starting in the 1960s, where the Singapore Government began setting rules and regulations for hawkers. One other large change is the government relocated these street vendors to a more permanent location instead.

To this day, Many Singaporeans flock to hawker centers on a daily basis to have their meals. This makes hawker centers incredibly crowded during peak hours Thus, it is difficult to carry one's meal from the vendor to a open table due to the large crowd at time.

List of Singaporean Food

Popular Chinese Food

Chicken Rice

One of the most popular and well know dish in Singapore. It is a dish consisted of poached chicken and seasoned rice, served with chili sauce and cucumber garnishes.


Hokkien Mee

It is a dish of egg noodles and rice noodles stir-fried with egg, pork, prawns and squid. It is usually served with sambal chilli, calamansi limes and on some occasions, small cubes of fried lard.


Bak Kut Teh

The name literally translating to "Meat Bone Tea" in Hokkien dialect, it is a dish consisting of pork ribs simmered in a broth with many herbs and spices in them.

Popular Malay/Indonesian Food

Nasi Goreng

A fried rice dish with a rich aromatic, earthy and smoky flavor. It is prepared with 3 components, the ingredients such as egg and meat, the seasoning such as chilli pepepr and spring onion, and lastly the condiments


Satay

A dish of seasoned, skewered and grilled meat, usually served with peanut sauce and a variety of other food. The meat can consist of chicken, pork, beef, lamb and so on, while the other foods served along side can consist of rice cake, cucumber and others.


Mee Rebus

Instead of having a regular soup base, this dish uses a spicy and sweet curry gravy instead. The noodles in the dish are yellow egg noodles.

Popular Indian Food

Roti Prata

An indian flatbread dish, served with many types of curry. This dish has a large amount of variety, as its ingredients can range from common ones such as meat or egg, and even more obscure ones such as banana or chocolate.


Indian Rojak

An assortment of different foods, such as potatoes, hard boiled eggs, tofu and prawn. It is also served with a sweet and spicy sauce made of peanuts and chilli.


Biryani

A mixed rice dish made with indian spices, vegetables, and some type of meat such as chicken, lamb or fish. It can be prepared in multiple ways, the two being "Pakki" which is cooked, and "Kacchi" which is raw.
